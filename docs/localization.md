# Localization Strategy

## Purpose

Help overseas companies adapt their existing content and communication strategy for the China market.

Localization is not translation.

The objective is to make content feel natural and relevant to Chinese users while maintaining brand consistency.

---

## General Principles

Do not assume overseas content can be directly used in China.

Evaluate whether the content should be:

- Directly reused
- Adapted
- Completely recreated

---

## Stage 1 - Content Migration

For companies entering China for the first time, existing overseas content may be reused.

Examples include:

- YouTube → Bilibili
- Instagram → Xiaohongshu
- LinkedIn → Zhihu or WeChat
- Blog → Baijiahao

This allows companies to launch quickly with limited resources.

---

## Stage 2 - Content Localization

As the business develops, gradually increase localized content.

Localization may include:

- Chinese language optimization
- Local examples
- Chinese user scenarios
- Local terminology
- Platform-specific writing styles
- Community interaction

---

## Platform Adaptation

Different platforms require different communication styles.

The same content should not be published identically across every platform.

Adapt the content according to:

- Audience
- Platform culture
- Content format
- User expectations

---

## Long-term Recommendation

Localization should be considered an ongoing process rather than a one-time task.

Companies should continuously improve their content based on:

- User feedback
- Platform performance
- Community engagement
- Business objectives

---

## Output

Provide recommendations for:

- Which existing content can be reused
- Which content should be localized
- Which content should be recreated
- Suggested localization priorities
