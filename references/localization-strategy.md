# Localization Strategy

Localization is not translation. The objective is to make the company feel relevant and credible to Chinese users while preserving brand consistency.

## Decision Framework

Evaluate each existing asset as:

- Reuse: publish with light edits when the content is already clear, evergreen, visual, or proof-based.
- Adapt: rewrite examples, titles, terminology, framing, or format for Chinese users and platform culture.
- Recreate: build new content when overseas assumptions, examples, humor, cultural references, or buyer concerns do not fit China.

## Stage 1: Content Migration

For first entry into China, existing overseas content can help launch quickly:

- YouTube to Bilibili
- Instagram to Xiaohongshu
- LinkedIn to Zhihu or WeChat
- Blog to Baijiahao, Zhihu, or WeChat
- Webinar recordings to Bilibili clips or WeChat article summaries

Use migration when resources are limited, but label it as a starting point rather than a mature China content strategy.

## Stage 2: Content Localization

Increase localized content as the business develops:

- Chinese language optimization
- Local examples and scenarios
- Local terminology
- Platform-specific writing style
- China-specific buyer objections
- Local case studies or proof points when available
- Community interaction and comment response

## Stage 3: China-Native Content

Recommend China-native content when:

- The product needs strong local trust.
- Overseas proof does not answer local buyer concerns.
- The category is competitive or platform culture is distinct.
- The company has local customers, partners, community members, or team members to feature.

## Platform Style Notes

- Zhihu: answer real questions, show expertise, compare options, avoid shallow promotional tone.
- WeChat: structured articles, official updates, educational series, event recaps, community nurturing.
- Bilibili: clear titles, chapters, demos, tutorials, practical value, authentic presenter style.
- Xiaohongshu: user-centered benefits, lifestyle context, visual clarity, trust-building tone.
- Douyin: fast hooks, simple demonstrations, strong visual payoff, concise CTA.
- Baijiahao: evergreen article structure, search-friendly titles, practical guidance.

## Output

Provide:

- Existing content to reuse
- Existing content to adapt
- Content to recreate for China
- Platform-specific localization notes
- Near-term localization priorities
